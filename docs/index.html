Research Projects

Automated Battery Swapping and Charging for Drone Swarm

The automated battery swapping and charging (ABSAC) project centers on building a fully automated battery swapping and charging system for drone swarms. I designed and fabricated a functional prototype, including modular components such as magnetized charging mounts and later spring-fit charging mounts and a forklift-style end-effector that also spring-fits onto batteries for carrying and placing. The control software—migrated from MicroPython to C—enables precise navigation and alignment during swapping operations has versions for both Raspberry Pi Pico and ESP32 (via PlatformIO) microcontrollers. The system has completed over 2,500 successful battery exchanges with no critical errors and is designed to manage up to 100 batteries continuously, performing a swap every 30 seconds. Supervised undergraduate research.

Contributions

  • Designed, fabricated, and programmed an automated HBot-based gantry system of 0.5m x 0.5m for continuous drone battery charging and swapping.
  • Designed, fabricated, and programmed an automated CoreXY-based gantry system of 1.5m x 1.5m for continuous drone battery charging and swapping.
  • Achieved 2,500+ successful swaps with zero critical errors; demonstrated 400 swaps in 8 hours at slow speed.
  • Designed and 3D-printed modular components including charging mounts for dead batteries, a forklift end-effector that spring-fits into batteries for carrying, and timing belt interfaces.
  • System supports 100 batteries with continuous operation and 30-second swap cycles.

Distributed Shape Formation and Control of Swarm

This project focuses on developing reconfigurable swarm robots capable of collective self-organization. I built a full-stack simulation framework for one-degree-of-freedom robots using differential programming, modeling real-world effects like magnetism and friction. Starting in Unity and later transitioning to DiffTaichi, I designed algorithms for shape identification, formation control, and lattice organization. To accelerate experimentation, I created headless simulations and gradient-descent optimization routines. I also contributed to hardware design—covering sensing, communication, and charging.

Contributions

  • Developed a differential-programming-based simulator for one-degree-of-freedom reconfigurable swarm robots, modeling magnetic and frictional interactions.
  • Built a Unity simulation framework for grid generation (random, lattice, seam, and row-displaced) and implemented distributed algorithms for shape identification and formation control.
  • Designed multiple annealing and sorting algorithms for lattice organization and boundary detection; transitioned simulations to DiffTaichi with a custom 2D physics engine.
  • Created a headless simulation mode to accelerate optimization and implemented gradient-based methods to tune motion parameters.
  • Contributed to physical robot design, including sensing, communication, and charging concepts
